1.EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The State University Construction Fund (“SUCF, Fund”), LeChase Construction (“LeChase”), Owner’s
Representative, and FAHS Construction Group, Inc. (“FAHS”), General Contractor, have remained committed
to working with Minority, Women and Service Disabled Veteran-owned (“MBE, WBE and SDVOB”) businesses
and fostering a diverse workforce throughout the Plant Science Building Renovation Project (Phase 1) at the
New York State College of Agriculture and Life Sciences at Cornell University. As the project approaches final
completion, the team continues to uphold these commitments through project closeout.
The scope of work included a phased gut renovation of the main building program spaces, incorporating new
floor plan layouts; installation of new mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and fire protection systems;
abatement of hazardous materials; window replacement; elevator upgrades; and improvements to the
building’s thermal and energy performance. The construction cost, based on the GC’s base contract, was
$49,339,000 with a $2,021,000 allowance. The project was awarded to FAHS in March 2023, and Phase I is on
track for completion in December 2025.
The Fund’s initial project goals were 15% MBE, 15% WBE, and 6% SDVOB participation. While a waiver was
granted by the Fund in recognition of the demonstrated good-faith efforts, the approved participation levels
reflect commitments of 7.76% MBE, 10.58% WBE, and 0.3% SDVOB.
Although no specific workforce diversity percentages were mandated, the Fund required the GC to
electronically report Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) workforce data and track workforce diversity
throughout the project. This reporting has been maintained through the final phases of construction.
To support, track, and monitor these goals, DACK Consulting Solutions, Inc. (“DACK”), a subconsultant to
LeChase, served as the Project’s MWBE Consultant in partnership with the project stakeholders through
substantial completion.
As of December 2025, and inclusive of change orders, the total contract value awarded to MWBE and SDVOB
firms increased to $10,131,180 with $9,514,434 eligible toward goal attainment (reflecting the 60% supplier
credit). This represents over 18.4% of the total contracts awarded on the project.
Significant progress has also been made in payments to MWBE firms. As of December 2025, $7,881,522
—approximately 77.8% of the total awarded value to MWBE/SDVOB firms—has been disbursed. Final
payments are anticipated to be processed in alignment with project closeout activities.
As the project concludes, the team remains committed to supporting diversity and inclusion through final
reporting and closeout, ensuring all participation and compliance requirements are fully documented and
satisfied.

2. GOALS
The Fund initially set project-specific participation goals of 15% Minority Business Enterprise (MBE), 15%
Women Business Enterprise (WBE), and 6% Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Business (SDVOB). These
goals were established to promote diversity and equitable access to contracting opportunities on the
project.
However, after an extensive review of the efforts made to engage and contract MWBE and SDVOB firms, the
Fund granted a waiver and approved a revised utilization plan that more accurately reflects the availability and
capacity of qualified firms in the marketplace.
•
•
•

MBE: 7.76% (down from 15%)
WBE: 10.58% (down from 15%)
SDVOB: 0.3% (down from 6%)

While the revised individual category goals have been adjusted, the overall combined MWBE utilization goal
remains strong, exceeding 18%. This figure reflects the total engagement of certified MWBE firms across
different contract categories, ensuring meaningful participation despite the waiver.
DACK played a crucial role in documenting, verifying, and justifying the waiver request by demonstrating good-faith
efforts to secure MWBE and SDVOB participation. This included:
•
•
•
•

Conducting outreach efforts to identify and engage eligible MWBE/SDVOB firms.
Monitoring contract awards to ensure MWBE firms were given equitable opportunities.
Reviewing utilization plans submitted by prime contractors and assessing their compliance.
Tracking payments to confirm that MWBE and SDVOB firms were compensated according to
contractual agreements.

Although the revised targets are lower than the initial goals, the Fund’s approval indicates that DACK and project
stakeholders made sufficient and documented efforts to meet the original objectives. The waiver approval
demonstrates an acknowledgment of the realities of subcontractor availability and reinforces the
commitment to achieving the highest feasible MWBE and SDVOB participation.
By maintaining transparency and compliance, DACK ensures that MWBE and SDVOB engagement remains a
priority, contributing to an inclusive and equitable procurement process for the project.

3. OUTREACH
To increase participation of MWBE/SDVOB firms in the project, DACK provided outreach during the bidding
phase. DACK uses a variety of methods to engage the MWBE community. We create informational e-blasts
providing project details; post on social media about the event; partner with organizations, chambers, and state
& civic organizations; develop master lists of contractors to target for the project; develop a central email
portal (MWBEOutreach@dackconsulting.com) to process all the MWBE contracting interests; organize
and coordinate Information Sessions to inform contractors about current and up-coming bidding opportunities.
In order to maximize the reach to MWBE community, DACK and LeChase held 2 events during the bidding
phase: In-person Walk-through Outreach and Virtual Meeting Outreach. No outreach events were held this
Quarter.

4. MWBE/SDVOB COMPLIANCE
As the MWBE/EEO Consultant for the Project, DACK plays a critical role in supporting and monitoring
compliance with Minority- and Women-Owned Business Enterprise (MWBE) and Service-Disabled VeteranOwned Business (SDVOB) goals. A key component of DACK’s responsibilities includes MWBE Compliance
Monitoring, which entails reviewing awarded contracts and assessing whether MWBE firms
are being utilized in accordance with established requirements.
DACK evaluates both the MWBE Utilization Plans submitted by prime contractors and the actual
contracts awarded to certified MWBE subcontractors and suppliers. This ensures not only that MWBEs
are being engaged in a meaningful way, but also that their participation aligns with New York State's
(NYS) standards and guidelines.
It’s important to note that, per Empire State Development (ESD) regulations, NYS-certified MWBE
suppliers coded under construction trades may only receive credit for 60% of any payments received. This credit
adjustment reflects the distinction between suppliers and direct service providers or
subcontractors, ensuring compliance data accurately represents MWBE participation in the project.
As of December 2025, total awards counted towards the goal to MWBE and SDVOB firms amount to $10,131,180
of total contract amount of $51,694,321.

6. WORKFORCE DIVERSITY
SUCF, LeChase, and FAHS are committed to advancing workforce diversity through the implementation of
a comprehensive Workforce Diversity Program, supported by policy statements and affirmative action
goals. This commitment ensures equal employment opportunities for racial/ethnic minorities and women on
the construction site, with a focus on fostering an inclusive work environment. To support this
initiative, DD is engaged as LeChase’s consultant, providing ongoing guidance and monitoring. This report
presents an update on the efforts and outcomes achieved during the third quarter of 2025, reflecting
progress toward the program's objectives.
In the Workforce Diversity Program, the hired contractors who assign employees to the sites are informed
that they shall take specific affirmative actions to ensure equal employment opportunity. FAHS is
committed to maintaining a daily log on-site to ensure compliance, with these log summaries being sent to
DACK to maintain records.
DACK meticulously monitored both the number of workers on-site and the hours logged by all workers on
site. A detailed spreadsheet was created to record the total hours worked by each employee, based on
the data from the daily logs. The information for the third quarter of 2025 is presented, along with
cumulative totals.
From October through December 2025, a total of 306 workers were active on the project site (108 in
October, 100 in November, and 98 in December).
During this period:
• 8 workers were identified as Black/African American (3 in October, 3 in November, 2 in December).
• 6 workers were identified as Hispanic/Latino (2 each month).
• 6 workers were identified as Native American/Indigenous (2 each month).
• 1 worker was identified as Asian/Pacific Islander (December).
• The majority of the workforce identified as Caucasian/White, with 101 workers in October, 93 in
November, and 91 in December.
• Female participation on site included 5 women in October, 4 in November, and 3 in December.
